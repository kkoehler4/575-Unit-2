How can I help?
    Plant milkweed.
    Planting milkweed in your garden or community spaces provides an essential habitat for the Western Monarch. In California, the best native milkweeds to plant are California milkweed, woollypod milkweed, and heartleaf milkweed.
    Avoid using pesticides.
    Both hercibides and insectides will kill monarch butterflies in their adult and caterpillar forms. Try to limit your pesticide use as much as possible.
    Reduce Lawn Space.
    Lawns don't provide any habitat for monarch butterflies. Instead, plant a variety of native blooming plants to provide nectar and habitat for butterflies.
    Volunteer.
    In California, you can help out with the annual Thanksgving Western Monarch Count that produced this very data! Other community science opportunities include the Journey North, the Southwest Monarch Study, and Project Monarch Health.
